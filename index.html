Race Summary

Incumbent Sheriff Rodney Wright is not seeking reelection (running for State Senate District 16). This is the first open Saline County Sheriff seat since 2014. No Democrat filed, making this Republican runoff the decisive election.

Four Republicans ran in the March 3 primary. None cleared 50%+1, triggering a runoff between the top two finishers.

Open Questions for Voters

  • Defamation lawsuit outcome — Allegations against Friend are serious but unproven in court. No findings of fact have been made.
  • Updated campaign finance — Pre-runoff filings (due ~March 24) not yet publicly accessible. Financial dynamics may have shifted.
  • Washington's 2,876 voters — Where the third-place finisher's supporters go could decide the runoff. No endorsement issued.
  • Friend's restructuring specifics — Says department is "too top heavy" but hasn't detailed which positions would be cut.
  • Robertson's distance from social media attacks — States his brother-in-law "acted independently." Voters may weigh whether further public response is warranted.
  • Boys on the Tracks file review — AR Division of LE Standards said it would review. No outcome reported.

Defamation Lawsuit Timeline

Friend filed suit; Robertson is not named but two defendants are connected to him.

January 20, 2026
Rick Cross (Robertson's brother-in-law) publishes letter on MySaline questioning Friend's handling of "Boys on the Tracks" case evidence.
February 3, 2026
Carl S. publishes rebuttal letter defending Friend, accusing Cross of deliberately omitting exculpatory documents.
Before March 3 (early voting)
Kevin Cooper posts 50+ minute Facebook video alleging Friend dismissed charges for sexual favors, stole property, and abused authority. Rick Cross posts claiming two protective orders existed against Friend.
March 3, 2026
Primary election. Robertson finishes first (37.3%), Friend second (32.9%). Runoff triggered.
March 10, 2026
Friend files defamation lawsuit in Saline County Circuit Court against Rick Cross, Kevin Cooper, and 5 John Does. Seeks $750,000+ in damages. Claims: defamation, false light invasion of privacy, tortious interference.
March 18, 2026
Cooper's attorney (Clint Lancaster) files motion to dismiss, arguing free speech and truth defense. Cross partially retracts claim about two protective orders — records show only one existed (filed 2014 by Friend's wife, dismissed for insufficient evidence).
March 26, 2026
Judge Brent Houston strikes Cooper's motion in its entirety, citing "name-calling" violations of Arkansas law in the filing. Threatens contempt if repeated.
Mid-April 2026
Deadline for Cross and remaining defendants to respond.
Attorney conflict: Friend's attorney (Jennifer Lancaster) and Cooper's attorney (Clint Lancaster) are concurrently divorcing each other (filed Aug 2025, hearings scheduled Aug 2026). All Saline County judges have recused from their divorce case.

Individual Controversies

Social Media Allegations Against Friend

Pending — Active Lawsuit

Cooper alleges Friend engaged in criminal conduct and professional misconduct. Cooper asserts the statements are true and that Friend bragged about the conduct to colleagues. Friend asserts the claims are fabricated and filed a $750K+ defamation suit. No findings of fact have been made.

Cooper is a Robertson supporter; Cross is Robertson's brother-in-law. Robertson states Cross "acted independently" and is not named in the suit. The timing (just before early voting) is relevant context for evaluating motivation.

Arkansas Times • KATV • MySaline

Boys on the Tracks Evidence Handling

Under Review

Cross raised concerns about Friend's handling of files from the unsolved 1987 Ives/Henry homicide case. Friend described on a podcast finding a state police file in 2004 while acting Shannon Hills police chief, taking materials home, then transferring them. AR Division of LE Standards said it would review. Cross stated he was not alleging criminal wrongdoing but questioning professional judgment.

This allegation also originated from Rick Cross (Robertson's brother-in-law). A rebuttal letter accused Cross of omitting exculpatory documents. No investigation outcome reported.

MySaline letter (Cross) • MySaline letter (rebuttal)

2014 Protective Order

Dismissed in 2014

A protective order was filed against Friend by his wife in 2014 and was dismissed/vacated for insufficient evidence. Cross amplified this on social media, falsely claiming two orders existed. He later retracted the second claim.

Arkansas Times

Campaign Dispute
